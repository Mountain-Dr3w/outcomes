# UI Decision Brief

For hiring reviewers in a fast portfolio scan, privilege credible proof and explicit project status over polished outcome language so Drew's actual seniority is legible without overstating unverified results

## At a glance

- **Primary outcome:** Within the homepage and first case-study viewport, the reviewer can distinguish measured impact, shipped capability, concept work, and validation still in progress
- **Primary action:** Choose a case study, understand its evidence, then continue to the next case or contact Drew
- **Selected model:** Evidence ledger
- **Product truth:** assumed
- **Residual product risk:** No hiring-manager usability study, analytics, or independent verification of project outcome claims is available

> Strong craft does not validate an unverified product premise.

## Situation

- **Person:** Design leaders, recruiters, and hiring managers evaluating senior product design and design leadership candidates
- **Moment:** A one-to-three-minute initial scan, usually before an interview, with deeper reading only if the first evidence is credible
- **Core job:** Judge Drew's level, individual contribution, ability to navigate complexity, and record of changing user or organizational outcomes
- **Consequence:** Unsupported claims make the portfolio's outcomes-first thesis feel promotional; specific status and proof make it trustworthy
- **Platform:** Responsive web, desktop-first hiring review with complete mobile adaptation

## Owned commitment

For hiring reviewers in a fast portfolio scan, privilege credible proof and explicit project status over polished outcome language so Drew's actual seniority is legible without overstating unverified results

- **Privilege:** Credibility, scanability, and senior decision evidence
- **Over:** Promotional compression and artifact volume
- **Owner:** Codex, reversible implementation judgment

## Refusals

- **F-001 / scope:** Do not invent a full Kessel Run case study without source artifacts or project-specific narrative — A fabricated or thin case would undermine the credibility problem this pass is meant to solve Protects: Truthfulness and Drew's professional reputation
- **F-002 / hierarchy:** Do not give concepts, shipped capabilities, and measured outcomes equal evidentiary weight — Reviewers need to distinguish what exists from what has been validated Protects: Accurate interpretation of Drew's impact
- **F-003 / aesthetic-behavior:** Do not replace the restrained editorial system with cards, ornamental dashboards, or decorative motion — The current visual language is coherent; the deficit is content hierarchy and evidence Protects: The portfolio's established identity and reading focus

## Direction decision

### R-001 — Evidence ledger

Keep the editorial homepage and case-study model, but make evidence level the repeating information spine: measured impact on the homepage, status and proof in each case, decisions paired with artifacts, and an honest end-state

- **Hierarchy:** Measured impact first, selected case studies second; within cases, status and strongest proof before narrative and imagery
- **Interaction:** Reviewers scan quantified impact, choose a case, follow decisions with adjacent artifacts, then continue or contact
- **Tradeoff:** Does not provide full narrative depth for older quantified work and keeps three current projects in the corpus
- **Why selected:** It fixes the audited credibility failure while preserving the portfolio's strongest asset: a restrained, coherent editorial identity. The flagship direction overweights an unmeasured case, while the taxonomy adds more categorization than this small corpus needs.

**Alternatives considered**

- **R-002 / Single flagship:** Make FORGE the dominant long-form case and reduce other work to short supporting entries
- **R-003 / Work taxonomy:** Split the homepage into proven impact, shipped products, and experiments, with different page treatments for each

## Visual fingerprint

- **Composition Rule:** Quantified proof occupies a stable leading column; each case moves from status and claim into decisions, with the artifact placed beside or immediately after the decision it supports
- **Type Voice:** Documentary and editorial: Lora carries reflective claims, Geist carries readable narrative, and Geist Mono labels status, provenance, and numbers
- **Color Role:** Near-black and warm neutrals create an archival field; rose marks orientation and provenance, while higher-contrast muted text preserves secondary hierarchy without sacrificing legibility
- **Material Model:** A continuous case-file publication separated by rules and artifact strips, not a dashboard or card collection
- **Imagery Icon Language:** Only project artifacts and product screens are used; images document decisions, use honest captions, preserve aspect ratio, and provide descriptive alternatives
- **Signature Behavior:** Restrained content reveal preserves reading cadence, while case-to-case navigation keeps the portfolio moving without decorative motion
